Kare-Kare


Image Source!
Ingredients
Measurements Ingredients
1 kg oxtail (or beef tripe, pork hock, or beef shank)
1 pc banana blossom (puso ng saging), sliced
1 pc bundle string beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch pieces
1 pc medium eggplant, sliced into rounds
1/2 cup peanut butter
1/4 cup ground toasted rice
1 pc medium onion, chopped
4 cloves garlic, minced
1/2 cup annatto water (soak annatto seeds in warm water and strain)
Instructions
  1. Boil the oxtail (or chosen meat) in 6 cups of water until tender, about 2–3 hours. You can use a pressure cooker to reduce cooking time. Skim off any scum that rises to the surface. Once tender, set aside the meat and broth.
  2. In a separate pot, blanch the string beans, eggplant, and banana blossom until tender but still crisp. Drain and set aside.
  3. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Sauté the garlic and onion until fragrant. Add the annatto water for color. Stir in the peanut butter and ground toasted rice, gradually adding the reserved broth to achieve a smooth, thick sauce. Mix well to avoid lumps.
  4. Add the boiled meat to the sauce and simmer for 10–15 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Arrange the cooked vegetables on top of the meat and sauce. Allow it to simmer for another 5 minutes.
  6. Serve hot with steamed rice and a side of shrimp paste (bagoong alamang). Enjoy your meal!
